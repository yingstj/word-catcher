# Word Catcher 📚⭐

A reading comprehension game for early readers. Kids decode words in a sentence, then catch the matching pictures as they rain down from the sky.

**[▶️ Play it](https://wordcatcher.julieyingst.com)**

---

## How it works

A sentence appears at the top of the screen — something like *"The apple falls from the tree onto the grass."* The target words (`apple`, `tree`, `grass`) are highlighted.

Then pictures start falling: 🍎 🌳 🌱 mixed in with distractors like 🍃 🐦 ☁️ ☀️ — items that fit the world but aren't in the sentence.

The child has to **read each word, hold its meaning, and tap the matching picture** when it falls. No labels on the pictures. No audio. Pure word-to-meaning-to-image decoding.

When all the target pictures are caught, the next sentence loads.

## Themed worlds

Each round takes place somewhere familiar:

🏫 School · 🏖️ Beach · 🌳 Backyard · 🍳 Kitchen · 🛏️ Bedroom · 🧸 Playroom · 🛝 Playground · 🦆 The Park

Finish all the sentences in a theme to unlock an animal friend. Collect them all.

## Design principles

Built for 5-year-olds who are just learning to sound out simple sentences.

- **No losing.** Wrong taps wobble and shake but never penalize.
- **No timer.** Items fall slowly and sway as they go.
- **No audio.** This is a reading comprehension exercise, not listening practice.
- **Visual feedback only.** When a target is caught, the word in the sentence flashes yellow and turns green — confirming the match without breaking flow.
- **Forgiving pace.** Items appear at a rhythm that doesn't overwhelm.

## Tech

A single HTML file. No build step, no dependencies beyond Google Fonts (Fraunces + Nunito). Works on phones, tablets, and laptops.
